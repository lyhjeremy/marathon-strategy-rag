# Carb-Loading Before Race Day
> Source: Established marathon training principles

Carb-loading maximizes your muscle and liver glycogen stores before the marathon, giving you more fuel in the tank before depletion sets in.

**The classic approach:**
- In the **1–3 days before the race**, raise carbohydrate intake substantially — commonly around **8–12 g of carbohydrate per kg of body weight per day**.
- Emphasize easily digested carbs: rice, pasta, bread, potatoes, oats, fruit, and sports drinks.
- Modern practice does **not** require the old "depletion phase" first; simply elevating carbs for a couple of days while tapering is effective and gentler.

**Practical tips:**
- **Lower fiber** in the final day or two (go for white rice/bread over heavy whole grains) to reduce GI bulk and race-morning bathroom issues.
- Don't just eat more of everything — **shift the ratio toward carbs** and hold protein/fat modest, rather than piling on a giant fatty feast.
- Expect a small weight gain (1–2 kg): glycogen binds water. This is normal and beneficial for hydration.

**What it is not:**
- It won't compensate for undertraining or a missing in-race fueling plan. Carb-loading fills the tank; in-race carbs keep topping it up.

**Actionable takeaway:** Over the final 1–3 days, increase carbohydrate intake, reduce fiber, keep portions manageable, and stick to familiar foods. The goal is full glycogen stores and a settled gut, not a dramatic last supper.
