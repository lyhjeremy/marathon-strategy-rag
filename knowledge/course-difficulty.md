# How Hard Is the Course? A Majors Difficulty Index
> Source: Jeremy Lee's World Marathon Majors course-difficulty analysis

Not all marathons are equally fast. A Course Difficulty Index across the six World Marathon Majors quantifies how much slower an equivalent runner will finish on each course, using **Berlin = 1.000** as the flat, fast baseline.

**The findings:**
- **Boston is the hardest (+101 seconds)** relative to Berlin for an equivalent runner.
- **New York City is next (+78 seconds).**
- **Berlin, Chicago, London, and Tokyo cluster tightly** — within about **35 seconds** of each other.

**Why Boston is slow despite being net downhill:**
- The Newton hills arrive around **30 km**, exactly where fatigue and glycogen depletion peak.
- Late-race climbing does far more damage than the same hills would early, so the net elevation drop doesn't translate into a fast time.

**What this means for you:**
- **Chasing a PR or a Boston qualifier?** Choose **Berlin, Chicago, or London** — flat, well-organized, and fast.
- **Racing Boston or NYC?** Expect a slower time and pace accordingly. Do not transfer a goal set on a flat course to a hard one.
- Factor course profile into your goal-setting the same way you factor in weather and fitness.

**Actionable takeaway:** Pick your race to match your goal. If the clock matters, target a flat major; if the experience or the challenge matters, embrace Boston or NYC and adjust your time expectations by a minute or more.
