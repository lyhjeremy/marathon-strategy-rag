# Tapering Before the Marathon
> Source: Established marathon training principles

The taper is the deliberate reduction of training in the final weeks so you arrive at the start line fresh, repaired, and fully adapted — without losing the fitness you built.

**The core approach:**
- Reduce **total volume by roughly 40–60%** over the final **2–3 weeks**, with the largest cut in race week.
- **Keep some intensity.** Maintain short segments at or near goal pace or with strides so your legs stay sharp and you don't feel flat.
- The key lever is **volume down, intensity mostly preserved** — this is what avoids detraining.

**Why it works:**
- Reduced training load lets muscle glycogen replenish, micro-damage repair, and accumulated fatigue dissipate.
- Fitness gains from hard training weeks are largely "banked" — you don't lose meaningful aerobic capacity in 2–3 weeks of reduced volume.

**Common mistakes:**
- **Over-tapering** (doing almost nothing) leaves you sluggish and anxious.
- **Under-tapering** (training hard too close to race day) leaves you tired on the start line.
- Trying to cram fitness in the final weeks — the work is already done; you can't improve it now, only ruin your freshness.

**Actionable takeaway:** Over the last 2–3 weeks, cut mileage substantially but keep a few crisp, race-pace efforts. Expect some restlessness and phantom aches — that's normal taper, not lost fitness. Trust the training you've already banked.
