# Race Week and Race Morning
> Source: Established marathon training principles

The training is done; race week is about arriving fresh, calm, and prepared. The golden rule: **nothing new on race day.**

**Race week:**
- Prioritize **sleep** across the whole week — the night *before* often brings pre-race nerves, so bank rest earlier.
- Follow your taper: low volume, a little sharpness.
- Sort **logistics** in advance: travel, bib pickup, start corral, gear, weather forecast, and a pacing plan adjusted for conditions and course.
- Carb-load and lower fiber in the final 1–3 days.

**Race morning:**
- **Pre-race meal:** eat a familiar, mostly-carb, low-fiber meal about **2–4 hours before** the start (e.g. oatmeal, toast, banana, sports drink). Give yourself time to digest and use the bathroom.
- Hydrate moderately; top up with a little sports drink, but don't overdrink.
- **Warm up** appropriately: for most amateurs a light jog and dynamic drills; faster racers do a fuller warm-up. Don't exhaust yourself before the gun.
- Lay out and test everything the night before: shoes, socks, apparel, chafing prevention, gels, and any anti-chafe or sun protection.

**The cardinal rule:**
- **No new shoes, foods, gels, clothing, or strategies on race day.** Everything should have been rehearsed in training and long runs.

**Actionable takeaway:** Sleep well across the week, dial in logistics early, eat a familiar carb-based meal 2–4 hours out, warm up modestly, and introduce absolutely nothing new. Preparation removes variables so you can focus on running.
