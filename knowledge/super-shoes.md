# Super Shoes: How Much Do Carbon Plates Really Help?
> Source: Jeremy Lee's elite-marathon performance-decomposition analysis

Carbon-plated "super shoes" arrived around 2017 and have been credited with a wave of record-breaking marathon times. But how much of that improvement is actually the shoes?

**What the decomposition shows:**
- Super shoes account for roughly **67 seconds** of the post-2017 elite gains.
- The 95% confidence interval is **36–99 seconds**.
- That's about **0.9% of a 2:05 marathon** — real and population-wide, but smaller than the headlines suggest.
- Other factors, especially **increasing depth of talent** in the elite field, also drove the improvement.

**What this means for you:**
- The benefit is **real but modest** — think tenths of a percent to about 1%, not a transformation.
- For an amateur, that might translate to a small handful of minutes over a marathon, roughly scaling with your finish time.
- Benefits vary by individual; some runners respond more than others, and fit and comfort still matter.

**Practical guidance:**
- Super shoes are **worth it for racing** if you want every legitimate advantage, but they are not magic and won't rescue undertrained fitness or poor pacing.
- They wear out faster and cost more, so many runners save them for goal races and hard workouts.
- Train enough in them beforehand to adapt; don't debut a new pair on race day.

**Actionable takeaway:** Buy super shoes for a small, real racing edge — then earn the rest with training, fueling, and pacing.
