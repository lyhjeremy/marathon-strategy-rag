# Are Boston Qualifying Standards Fair?
> Source: Jeremy Lee's BQ standards fairness analysis

Boston Marathon qualifying (BQ) times are set by age and gender bracket. A common question: are these standards fair across groups? The analysis of 22 age-gender brackets gives a nuanced answer.

**The findings:**
- **No mean-level gender bias** — on average, men's and women's standards are comparably difficult.
- But **women's brackets are 3–4× more variable in difficulty** than men's. How hard your BQ is depends heavily on *which* bracket you fall into.
- The **W80+ bracket is roughly 57 minutes too strict** — a clear outlier that is far harder to achieve than its counterparts.
- The **2026 tightening** of standards addressed **demand** (too many qualifiers for available slots), **not fairness** across brackets.

**What this means for you:**
- Don't assume your BQ is "the same difficulty" as someone else's in a different age or gender bracket. The spread is real, especially among women's and older-age brackets.
- If you're in a bracket that happens to be relatively lenient, you may have an easier path than the raw times suggest; if you're in a strict outlier, recognize the target is genuinely tougher.
- Tightening standards changes who gets in, but it does not fix the uneven difficulty *between* brackets.

**Actionable takeaway:** Know your exact age-and-gender bracket and treat its standard on its own terms. Set your training target to the specific number you must hit, and understand that "how hard a BQ is" is bracket-dependent, not universal.
